Build Challenge

Build an agent that knows when to stop.

One challenge. Two artifacts. We want to see how you think — not how much you can ship.

The Challenge

Build a system that researches questions and answers them well.

Given a research question, it produces a sourced answer. Beyond that, the design is yours.

Here is what the system must do. The how is the part we are interested in.

01

It decides on its own when it has enough evidence.

No hardcoded step counts. The system has to reason about whether it is done, and the reasoning has to be defensible.

02

Every completed run is fully inspectable.

What the system did, what it found, what it concluded, and why it stopped — all visible after the fact, to you or to someone else.

03

A run can be re-examined and re-attempted.

If the approach turned out to be wrong, someone should be able to go back to a point in the run and try a different path from there.

04

It handles messy reality.

Ambiguous questions. Sources that contradict each other. Sources that return nothing. The system should not pretend these don't happen.

What to submit

Two artifacts. In order.

We want your thinking before your code. Submit the design doc first. Build once it is sent.

Due · 24 hours from now
Design doc
One page. Your architecture, your data model, how your system decides to stop, and the single thing you are most worried about. Written in your voice, not generated.
Due · within one week
Working build
The running system plus source code. Add a short note on anything that changed from your design doc and why it changed.
The review

35 minutes. Two halves.

Once both artifacts are in, we will schedule a conversation. It is structured so that the first half is yours to show your work, and the second half is us working together on something new.

00 · 15

Demo

We will bring three research questions we have chosen in advance. You will run them live against your system and walk us through what happens.

15 · 35

Pair session

We will introduce a new requirement we did not ask for in the brief. We will work through how you would add it to your system, together.

Ground rules

A few things to know.

Stack
Any language, framework, database, or model. Your choice, entirely.
AI assistance
Welcome. Use whatever tools you use. Be ready to defend every decision.
Time
Target 4–6 focused hours on the build itself. Submit within one week of the design doc.
Polish
Not the point. Clarity of thought is. Rough edges are fine. Muddled reasoning is not.
Why this challenge

What we're really looking at.

The classic full-stack interview test asks a candidate to shuffle a deck of cards with a real random number generator, pull the top card, and report how many shuffles it takes to draw the same card again. It works because it forces a single piece of work to travel cleanly through every architectural boundary. You cannot fake it.

This is our version for the kind of work we actually do. It is deliberately open-ended. We are not looking for the right answer — we are looking at how you frame the problem, which tradeoffs you choose to make, and how you defend them when we push back.

Good luck. We're looking forward to seeing what you make.
